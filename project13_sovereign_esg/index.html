Sovereign ESG and Bond Spreads

Do Country-Level ESG Metrics Predict Sovereign Borrowing Costs?

Languages: Python & R Category: Financial Analytics
Research Question Do improvements in sovereign ESG indicators (governance, environmental policy, social outcomes) reduce government bond yield spreads?
Methodology

Panel regression with country and year fixed effects, using World Bank governance indicators, environmental performance indices, and social development metrics as predictors of sovereign bond spreads over US Treasuries.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: World Bank API (wbgapi), IMF WEO, Worldwide Governance Indicators
Countries: 40+ emerging and developed markets
Variables: Bond spread, WGI governance scores, CO2 per capita, HDI, GDP growth, debt/GDP
Key finding: Governance improvements predict lower spreads (t = −2.28)
Key Methods
Country Fixed Effects
Controls for all time-invariant country characteristics (legal system, culture, geography)
World Bank API (wbgapi)
Programmatic access to WDI indicators — enables reproducible, up-to-date sovereign ESG data collection
Driscoll-Kraay Standard Errors
Robust to cross-sectional dependence and heteroskedasticity — critical for sovereign panel data
Key Findings
1
Governance improvements (WGI composite) predict lower sovereign bond spreads (t = −2.28, p < 0.05)
2
Environmental metrics (CO2 per capita) show no significant spread effect after controlling for GDP — environmental risk not yet priced in sovereign debt
3
Social indicators (HDI, inequality) have marginal significance — the Governance pillar dominates the ESG–spread relationship
